Default rate vs expected loss by internal rating tier

BBB is the largest segment (1,556 loans) with a 9.4% default rate and $8.89M expected loss — 42% of total portfolio EL from one tier. Recommend tightening BBB criteria (score ≥ 700 + DTI < 2.0x).

Portfolio distribution by internal rating

Investment-grade tiers (AAA–BBB) represent 82.6% of portfolio. Speculative-grade (BB–CCC) at 17.4% — within typical bank limits of 15–20%.
Portfolio is well diversified across investment-grade tiers. BB/B/CCC exposure is manageable but warrants ongoing monitoring as economic conditions change.

Default rate by loan purpose

Business loans show the lowest default rate (5.1%). Personal loans are highest at 8.3% — shifting origination mix toward business lending could reduce overall portfolio default rate.

Expected loss by loan purpose ($M)

Despite lower default rate, Home loans carry the highest EL ($3.69M) due to larger loan sizes. Business and Debt Consolidation EL is driven more by volume than default rate.

Stress scenario loss by rating ($M)

99th percentile loss estimate per tier — not VaR in the trading sense. Represents stressed credit loss under adverse scenario.
A-rated and BBB-rated tiers drive the largest stress exposure ($28M and $36M) — not because they have high default rates, but because they hold the most loans.

ERM risk concentration — estimated portfolio exposure

Market risk stress scenario ($97.6M) dominates ERM exposure due to portfolio size. Credit EL ($21.4M) is well-controlled at 3.28% of portfolio.

Credit score distribution across portfolio

Score distribution is roughly bell-shaped with a peak in the 680–740 range — consistent with a near-prime consumer portfolio. Subprime (<580) loans represent a small tail.
